Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how BitGuard handles information when you use our website and related services.

Last updated: March 28, 2026

1. Overview

BitGuard is designed to provide Bitcoin wallet risk assessment and related informational services. We value user privacy and aim to limit the collection of personal information to what is reasonably necessary to operate and improve the service.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect limited information that you voluntarily provide to us, such as information submitted through our contact form. This may include your name, email address, and any information you choose to include in your message.

We may also collect technical or usage-related information automatically when you access the site, such as browser type, device information, general usage logs, and similar diagnostic data that help us maintain site security, performance, and reliability.

3. How We Use Information

We may use collected information to:

  • operate, maintain, and improve BitGuard;
  • respond to questions, requests, and support inquiries;
  • monitor performance, reliability, and security;
  • comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms and policies.

4. Wallet Addresses and Risk Assessments

BitGuard processes Bitcoin wallet addresses and related blockchain-derived signals in order to generate risk assessments. Blockchain data is generally public by nature. BitGuard does not treat public wallet addresses as confidential personal information solely because they are submitted for analysis, although we still seek to handle submitted data responsibly and securely.

5. Cookies and Tracking

BitGuard does not currently use advertising cookies or third-party behavioral tracking technologies for marketing purposes. We may use limited technical tools, analytics, or basic session-related functionality as needed to support site operation, security, and performance.

6. Sharing of Information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share limited information with service providers or infrastructure providers that help us host, maintain, or secure the service, but only to the extent reasonably necessary for those purposes. We may also disclose information if required by law, regulation, legal process, or to protect rights, safety, and security.

7. Data Retention

We retain information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, including to provide the service, address support requests, maintain security, resolve disputes, and comply with legal obligations.

8. Data Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or alteration. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Third-Party Links and Services

Our website may contain links to third-party websites or services, including external forms or resources. We are not responsible for the privacy, security, or content practices of third parties. You should review the privacy policies of those third-party services before providing information.

10. Children's Privacy

BitGuard is not directed to children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe that a child has provided personal information through the service, please contact us so we can review and address the issue.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we may revise the effective date above and post the updated version on this page. Your continued use of BitGuard after any changes become effective constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

12. Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us through our contact page.
